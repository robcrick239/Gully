Sage

Scientific Name

The scientific name for sage is "Salvia officinalis"

Origin

Sage is native to the Mediterranean region.

Health Benefits

Sage contains antioxidant vitamins A, C, and E in small amounts.

Sage has been shown to have antimicrobial effects that aid in killing plaque.

Sage is used for treating Alzheimer disease, diabetes, high cholesterol, and symptoms of menopause.

Fun Facts!

Some fun facts about sage are:

In medieval Europe, sage was thought to strengthen the memory and promote wisdom.

During the 17th century, the Chinese exchanged three or four pounds of their tea with Dutch traders for one pound of European sage leaves.

Sage is considered a good rodent repellent.
