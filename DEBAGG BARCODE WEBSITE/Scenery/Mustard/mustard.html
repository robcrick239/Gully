Mustard

Scientific Name

The scientific name for Mustard varies based on the type of mustard, however the overall mustard is scientifically identified as “Brassica”. For yellow or white mustard, the scientific name is“Sinapis alba”. For brown and oriental mustards, “ Brassica juncea”. For field mustard, the scientific name is “Brassica rapa”.

Origin

The yellow or white mustard is of Mediterranean origin. The brown, or Indian, mustard (Brassica juncea), is of Himalayan origin. Mustard has ties to ancient Egypt as well as parts of Europe and Asia.

Health Benefits

Mustard has many health benefits and some are:

•Mustard provides relief for migraine attacks

•Mustard oil is known to be great for the skin

•A weight loss supplement, asthma suppressant, hair growth stimulant, immunity booster, cholesterol regulator, dermatitis treatment, and even as an effective method of warding off gastrointestinal cancer

Fun Facts!

Some fun facts about mustard are:

Egyptian pharaohs stocked their tombs with mustard seeds to accompany them into the afterlife

Mustard plants are close relatives to a surprising variety of common vegetables, including broccoli, cauliflower, turnips, and cabbage.
