Nutmeg

Scientific Name

The scientific name for nutmeg is "Myristica fragrans"

Origin

Many wanted to find the mysterious “Spice Islands” where nutmeg was grown. In 1512, the Spanish finally discovered the location in the Banda Islands where all the world’s nutmeg grew.

Health Benefits

Nutmeg does not have any real, proven medicinal benefits. In fact, nutmeg ingested in large amounts is poisonous.

However, nutmeg can assist in some cases with bad breath, indigestion, depression and sleeping

Fun Facts!

Some fun facts about nutmeg are:

The fruit/flesh of the nutmeg seed is actually edible

During the 17th century, the Dutch waged a bloody war in order to gain control of the nutmeg production.

Emperor Henry VI spread nutmeg all over the streets of Rome before his coronation to create a sweet smelling environment.

Nutmeg was fashionable among the wealthy because it was believed to induce hallucinations.
